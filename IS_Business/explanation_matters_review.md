## IS / Business Literature Example: Explanation Matters

**Bibliographic Information**
- **Title**: Explanation matters: How explainable artificial intelligence can improve decision-making in human-AI interaction
- **Authors**: Hamm et al.
- **Year**: 2023
- **Venue**: Electronic Markets
- **Research Topic**: human-AI interaction; explainability; decision-making
- **Note**: A business-facing empirical paper on whether explanations improve decisions.

This paper directly addresses a question that matters for business journals: do explanations actually improve decision-making in human-AI interaction? Its contribution lies in moving beyond abstract claims about transparency and examining explanation as a practical determinant of decision quality.

## Research Logic Chain
- **Problem gap**: Many XAI claims assume explanations are beneficial.
- **Why prior work was insufficient**: The decision consequences of explanation were not always tested directly.
- **Core idea**: Evaluate whether explainability changes human decision quality and interaction with AI.

## Key Terms
- **Decision quality**: how good decisions become when AI support is used.
- **Human-AI interaction**: the joint process in which people use AI outputs.
- **Explainability effect**: the behavioral effect of receiving explanations.

## Data / Research Setting
This is an empirical business-oriented study focused on decision behavior rather than model benchmarking.

## Benchmark / Evaluation Logic
The central evaluation logic is experimental: compare conditions with and without explanations and observe how decision outcomes change.

## Performance Comparison
The paper's main finding is that explanation matters because it changes how users interact with AI and how effectively they make decisions.

## Architecture / Conceptual Explanation

Read the paper as a behavioral evaluation chain:
- `Input`: explanation design choices.
- `Core mechanism`: explanations affect understanding and calibrated reliance.
- `Key message`: explanation quality should be judged by downstream human decision quality.
## How the Main Metrics / Evaluation Logic Work
The main logic is behavioral: explanation quality should be judged by whether it improves user decisions, not only by whether it sounds plausible.

## Relevance to the Present Project Design
This paper strongly supports the idea that explanation quality in the present project should be evaluated by downstream usefulness for managers or analysts, not only by technical appearance.
