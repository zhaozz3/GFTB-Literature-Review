## IS / Business Literature Example: Human-Centered XAI Evaluation

**Bibliographic Information**
- **Title**: Human-centered evaluation of explainable AI applications: a systematic review
- **Authors**: Nothdurft et al.
- **Year**: 2024
- **Venue**: Frontiers in Artificial Intelligence
- **Research Topic**: human-centered XAI evaluation; explanation usefulness; evaluation dimensions
- **Note**: A recent review focused on how explainable AI should be evaluated from a human-centered perspective.

This review argues that explainable AI cannot be evaluated adequately through technical accuracy metrics alone. Instead, explanation quality must be assessed in relation to user goals, decision contexts, perceived usefulness, trust, and actionability. The paper is especially important for business and IS research because it gives a language for evaluating AI artifacts as decision-support tools rather than only predictive engines.

## Research Logic Chain
- **Problem gap**: XAI evaluation often remains model-centric.
- **Why prior work was insufficient**: Technical metrics do not capture human usefulness.
- **Core idea**: Organize XAI evaluation around user-centered criteria and application context.
- **Implementation chain**:
  1. Review empirical XAI studies;
  2. Classify evaluation dimensions;
  3. Compare human-centered measures;
  4. Derive implications for future artifact evaluation.

## Key Terms
- **Human-centered evaluation**: judging AI systems relative to user needs and outcomes.
- **Actionability**: whether an explanation supports action.
- **Explanation usefulness**: whether users can employ the explanation meaningfully.

## Data / Research Setting
This is a systematic review rather than an algorithm paper. Its research setting is the corpus of prior XAI application studies across domains.

## Benchmark / Evaluation Logic
The central evaluation logic is conceptual: explanations should be judged not only by plausibility or technical fidelity, but also by user understanding, trust, satisfaction, and decision quality.

## Performance Comparison
Instead of benchmark tables, the paper contributes an evaluation synthesis. Its main contribution is identifying recurring human-centered dimensions that should complement technical metrics.

## Architecture / Conceptual Explanation

Read the paper as a human-centered evaluation frame:
- `Inputs`: explanation, user, and task are co-determinants.
- `Core mechanism`: explanation quality is context-dependent rather than absolute.
- `Key message`: prediction quality alone is an incomplete evaluation target.
## How the Main Metrics / Evaluation Logic Work
The main logic is multi-dimensional evaluation: explanation quality is meaningful only in relation to user comprehension, task support, trust, and behavioral consequences.

## Relevance to the Present Project Design
This paper is highly relevant because the present project cannot be evaluated on prediction quality alone. It supports adding human-usefulness, actionability, and explanation-quality criteria to the evaluation design.
