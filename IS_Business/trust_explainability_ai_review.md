## IS / Business Literature Example: Trust, Explainability and AI

**Bibliographic Information**
- **Title**: Trust, Explainability and AI
- **Author**: Baron
- **Year**: 2025
- **Venue**: AI & Society
- **Research Topic**: trust; explainability; normative framing of AI use
- **Note**: A recent conceptual paper on the relationship between trust and explainability in AI systems.

This paper examines the relationship between trust and explainability in AI. Its relevance to business and IS work is that it cautions against treating explanation as an automatic solution: explanations may support trust, but trust depends on context, expectations, and institutional framing. This makes it useful for thinking carefully about how explanation should be positioned in business artifacts.

## Research Logic Chain
- **Problem gap**: Explainability is often assumed to produce trust automatically.
- **Why prior work was insufficient**: Trust is more complex than transparency alone.
- **Core idea**: Clarify how trust and explanation are related conceptually and normatively.

## Key Terms
- **Trust**: the willingness to rely on a system under uncertainty.
- **Explainability**: the capacity to render AI behavior understandable.
- **Normative framing**: evaluating what explanations ought to achieve, not only what they technically show.

## Data / Research Setting
This is a conceptual paper rather than an empirical benchmark study.

## Benchmark / Evaluation Logic
Its logic is philosophical and organizational: it asks what role explanation should play in trustworthy AI use.

## Performance Comparison
The paper contributes conceptual clarification rather than benchmark results.

## Architecture / Conceptual Explanation

Read the paper as a trust-calibration model:
- `Input`: explanation is one contributor, not the whole story.
- `Core mechanism`: trust is shaped jointly by explanation and context.
- `Key message`: better explanation does not automatically imply better trust.
## How the Main Metrics / Evaluation Logic Work
The central logic is that trust should not be inferred from explanation alone; organizational and human context matter.

## Relevance to the Present Project Design
This paper is useful because the present project also risks over-assuming that explanation automatically creates confidence or usefulness. It supports a more careful framing of explanation as one contributor to trust rather than a guaranteed solution.
