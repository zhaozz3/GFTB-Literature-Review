## IS / Business Literature Example: Electronic Markets XAI Bibliometric Review

**Bibliographic Information**
- **Title**: Electronic markets information systems with explainable artificial intelligence: A bibliometric analysis of publications from 2000 to 2024
- **Authors**: Suleiman Adamu, Aamo Iorliam, Ozcan Asilkan
- **Year**: 2025
- **Venue**: *Global Journal of Information Technology: Emerging Technologies*, 15(1), 1-15
- **DOI**: 10.18844/gjit.v15i1.9677
- **Research Topic**: bibliometric analysis; XAI in electronic markets; literature mapping
- **Note**: This is a bibliometric and systematic mapping paper rather than an empirical XAI method paper.

This paper maps the literature at the intersection of electronic markets, information systems, and explainable AI. Its contribution is not algorithmic innovation, but a quantitative and descriptive overview of how this small research niche is forming.

## Research Logic Chain
- **Problem gap**: XAI research in electronic markets is fragmented and still very new.
- **Why prior work was insufficient**: Researchers lacked a concrete map of themes, sources, and publication patterns in this subfield.
- **Core idea**: Use bibliometric analysis plus a structured review process to chart the field.

## Key Terms
- **Bibliometric analysis**: quantitative mapping of a literature.
- **Topic cluster**: a recurring theme in a body of publications.
- **Electronic markets**: digitally mediated market environments.
- **PRISMA**: a structured procedure for systematic identification, screening, and inclusion of literature.

## Data / Research Setting

This paper is based on a literature corpus rather than on model-training data. The search covers **2000-2024** and uses:
- **Google Scholar**
- **Crossref**
- **Scopus**

The paper reports the following search and screening process:
- **1,932 records identified** in total
- after screening and eligibility filtering, **12 publications** were finally included
- the included set was analyzed with **VOSviewer 1.6.20**

The database-level retrieval counts reported in `Table 2` are:
- Google Scholar: `860 + 64`
- Crossref: `1000`
- Scopus: `8`

This is a small final sample, but that is itself one of the paper’s findings: the topic is still very new and thinly populated.

## Benchmark / Evaluation Logic

This is not an empirical benchmark paper, so it does not report AUC, accuracy, or model-comparison tables. Its evaluation logic is bibliometric and descriptive:
1. how many relevant publications exist
2. where they are published
3. how publication volume evolves over time
4. which keywords and themes dominate
5. which works are most cited

## Performance Comparison

The main findings are concrete:
- the earliest relevant publications in this niche appear only around **2023**
- the number of included publications rises to **8 in 2024**, versus **4 in 2023**
- **Springer** accounts for **7 of the 12** included publications, making it the dominant publisher in the final sample
- the most cited work in the included set is **Khan and Naim (2024)** with **4 citations**
- **Ansari et al. (2023)** and **Kasimu et al. (2023)** each have **2 citations**
- the most frequent keywords include **“electronic commerce”** and **“explainable artificial intelligence”**

So the contribution is not “this paper beats another model”, but rather that it quantitatively demonstrates how young, small, and publisher-concentrated this niche currently is.

## Architecture / Conceptual Explanation

Read the paper as a literature-processing pipeline:
- `Input`: broad database retrieval.
- `Core mechanism`: screening narrows the corpus before mapping and interpretation.
- `Key message`: the contribution is a structured field map, not a new XAI method.
## How the Main Metrics / Evaluation Logic Work

The evaluation logic is bibliometric:
- publication counts describe field size
- keyword frequency describes theme concentration
- citation counts describe local influence
- publisher/source distribution describes outlet structure

This is appropriate because the paper’s aim is field mapping, not methodological validation.

## Relevance to the Present Project Design

This paper is useful mainly for **field positioning**. It helps argue that explainable AI in electronic-market and commerce settings is an emerging but still underdeveloped area. At the same time, its small final sample of **12 publications** also warns against overstating the maturity of the field.
