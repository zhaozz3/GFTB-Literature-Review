## IS / Business Literature Example: Explainable and Responsible AI

**Bibliographic Information**
- **Title**: Explainable and responsible artificial intelligence
- **Authors**: Meske et al.
- **Year**: 2022
- **Venue**: Electronic Markets
- **Research Topic**: XAI; responsibility; business and platform contexts
- **Note**: A business-facing conceptual paper connecting explainability with responsible AI.

This paper links explainability to the broader responsible-AI agenda. It argues that explanation is not only a usability feature but also part of accountability, governance, and responsible organizational deployment. For business and IS work, the article is useful because it broadens the justification for explanation beyond technical transparency.

## Research Logic Chain
- **Problem gap**: XAI and responsible AI were often discussed separately.
- **Why prior work was insufficient**: Organizational responsibility requires more than model-level interpretability.
- **Core idea**: Position explanation as one dimension of responsible AI in digital and market settings.

## Key Terms
- **Responsible AI**: AI aligned with accountability, fairness, and governance requirements.
- **Explainability**: the capacity to make AI behavior understandable.
- **Governance**: the organizational structures through which AI use is controlled.

## Data / Research Setting
This is a conceptual and positioning paper rather than an empirical benchmark study.

## Benchmark / Evaluation Logic
The contribution is conceptual integration: it evaluates how explainability should be understood within a larger responsible-AI framework.

## Performance Comparison
No benchmark table is involved. The paper's value lies in theory-building and managerial framing.

## Architecture / Conceptual Explanation

Read the paper as a governance ladder:
- `Starting point`: an AI system is not judged only by performance.
- `Core mechanism`: explainability supports accountability and responsible use.
- `Key message`: governance capability is part of the artifact's value proposition.
## How the Main Metrics / Evaluation Logic Work
The logic is normative and organizational: an AI artifact should be judged partly by whether it supports accountability and responsible use.

## Relevance to the Present Project Design
This paper supports framing the present project not only as explainable AI, but as a responsible decision-support artifact whose explanations have governance and accountability value.
