## IS / Business Literature Example: Explainable Artificial Intelligence in Information Systems

**Bibliographic Information**

- **Title**: Explainable artificial intelligence in information systems: A review of the status quo and future research directions
- **Authors**: Julia Brasse, Hanna Rebecca Broder, Maximilian Förster, Mathias Klier, Irina Sigler
- **Year**: 2023
- **Venue**: *Electronic Markets*, 33, Article 26
- **DOI**: 10.1007/s12525-023-00644-5
- **Research Topic**: explainable AI; information systems; structured literature review; electronic markets; research agenda; IS contribution to XAI
- **Recommended APA Citation**: Brasse, J., Broder, H. R., Förster, M., Klier, M., & Sigler, I. (2023). Explainable artificial intelligence in information systems: A review of the status quo and future research directions. *Electronic Markets, 33*, 26. https://doi.org/10.1007/s12525-023-00644-5
- **Note**: This is a structured review article in the IS domain. Its core value lies in systematically mapping the development of XAI in Information Systems and identifying future research opportunities, especially in electronic markets.

The central purpose of this paper is not to propose a new explainable AI method, but to answer a question that is particularly relevant for IS research: **what has IS research already contributed to XAI, what gaps remain, and what opportunities exist especially in electronic markets?** Brasse et al. (2023) argue that as AI becomes increasingly embedded in firms and market environments, the opacity of black-box models directly affects human-AI interaction, trust formation, accountability, and system adoption. Because IS research naturally combines technical, organizational, user, and market perspectives, it is especially well positioned to make distinctive contributions to XAI. Based on this premise, the paper conducts a structured literature review of XAI in IS and develops a future research agenda from that review.

From an innovation perspective, the contribution of the paper lies not in algorithms, but in **knowledge organization and field mapping**. The authors identify **180 research papers** from **105 journals** and **17 conferences**. They do not merely count publications; they systematically analyze:

- receptive outlets
- the academic discussion over time
- underlying concepts and methodologies
- **8 research areas** in the context of electronic markets

Thus, the paper’s key value is that it organizes a previously scattered body of XAI-in-IS research into a coherent overview and clearly highlights the unique opportunities for IS research in explainability, human-AI interaction, decision support, electronic markets, and governance.

## Research Logic Chain

- **Problem gap**: XAI research is expanding rapidly, but its development path, core concepts, methodological distribution, and future opportunities within IS remain unclear.
- **Why prior work was insufficient**: General XAI surveys tend to focus more on algorithmic aspects and do not sufficiently reveal the distinctive role of IS research in organizational, user, market, and digital-platform settings.
- **Core idea of the paper**: Use a structured literature review to systematically map the current state of XAI in IS and derive major research areas and future directions.
- **Implementation chain**:
  1. Define the intersection between XAI and IS;
  2. Design a keyword-based search strategy;
  3. Select papers from IS-related journals and conferences;
  4. Analyze outlets, temporal development, concepts, and methodologies;
  5. Derive major research areas in electronic markets;
  6. Build a future research agenda based on the review.
- **Validation logic**: As a structured literature review, the paper establishes validity through systematic search, classification, and synthesis rather than experimental benchmarking.

## Key Terms

- **XAI (Explainable AI)**: the field concerned with helping humans understand how AI systems produce predictions, recommendations, and decisions.
- **IS (Information Systems)**: a field concerned with the interaction of technology, organizations, users, and business processes.
- **Structured literature review**: a review method based on systematic search and structured analysis rather than only narrative summary.
- **Electronic markets**: digital-market or platform-based contexts, which are one of the focal application settings of the paper.
- **Interpretability**: whether an AI system can be understood from a human perspective.
- **Explainability**: the capability of a system to produce or support explanations.
- **Human-AI interaction**: how humans understand, trust, use, and correct AI systems.
- **Research agenda**: a structured set of future research directions derived from current gaps.

The “data” in this paper are not training instances, but the literature corpus identified through the structured review. The final review corpus includes:

- **180 research papers**

The search strategy uses keyword combinations related to explainable / comprehensible AI and machine learning in order to capture the relevant XAI literature in IS.

From a research design perspective, this review setting is important because it makes the paper more than just an opinion piece. Its arguments are grounded in a relatively systematic and traceable literature corpus. For your own paper, this is especially useful because it can serve as a credible source for describing the overall status of XAI in IS.

## Benchmark / Evaluation Logic

This paper is not an empirical algorithm paper, so it does not report AUC, accuracy, or benchmark tables. Its “evaluation logic” instead concerns how a research field is assessed:

1. Which journals and conferences are most receptive to XAI in IS?
2. How has the discussion of XAI in IS evolved over time?
3. Which concepts and methodologies dominate the field?
4. Which research areas in electronic markets are already mature, and which remain underdeveloped?
5. Which future directions are most promising for IS scholars?

In other words, the paper evaluates the field in terms of:

- maturity
- structure
- development path
- research gaps

This is especially useful because a literature review often needs not only methods, but also a way to locate a study within the broader IS/business literature.

## Review Logic

Because this paper is not an empirical benchmark study, it does not evaluate model performance. Instead, its review logic is field-oriented. The central questions are:

1. How mature is XAI research within IS?
2. Which concepts, methods, and outlets dominate the field?
3. Which application areas in electronic markets are already well developed, and which remain underexplored?
4. What distinctive contribution can IS research make beyond algorithmic XAI work?

This is useful because it clarifies how IS scholarship approaches explainability differently from purely technical surveys: the emphasis is not only on model transparency, but also on organizational context, user interaction, market settings, adoption, and governance.

## Literature Value and Boundaries

The main value of this paper is as a **field-positioning, concept-clarification, and gap-identification source** within the IS/business literature. It is especially helpful when a review needs to explain how explainability research fits into Information Systems rather than treating XAI only as a computer-science topic.

At the same time, its boundaries are clear. As a structured review, it does not provide a technical method for explanation generation, prompt optimization, or error-to-text feedback design. Its contribution is conceptual and organizational rather than algorithmic.
