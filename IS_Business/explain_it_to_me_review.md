## IS / Business Literature Example: Expl(AI)n It to Me

**Bibliographic Information**
- **Title**: Expl(AI)n It to Me - Explainable AI and Information Systems Research
- **Authors**: Bauer et al.
- **Year**: 2021
- **Venue**: Business & Information Systems Engineering
- **Research Topic**: XAI in IS; research agenda; explanation and organizational use
- **Note**: A conceptual IS-oriented paper connecting explainable AI to information systems research.

This paper translates explainable AI into IS language. Rather than asking only how a model becomes interpretable, it asks how explanations shape organizational use, decision support, accountability, and user interaction. Its contribution is not a new algorithm but a framing device for XAI as an information-systems research problem.

## Research Logic Chain
- **Problem gap**: XAI discussions were dominated by technical communities.
- **Why prior work was insufficient**: IS questions about use, governance, and organizational value were underdeveloped.
- **Core idea**: Position XAI as a socio-technical IS phenomenon and derive a research agenda.

## Key Terms
- **Socio-technical system**: a system shaped by both technical and human elements.
- **Accountability**: whether decisions can be justified and traced.
- **IS framing**: describing a technical artifact in terms of users, organizations, and processes.

## Data / Research Setting
This is a conceptual research-agenda paper rather than an empirical benchmark study.

## Benchmark / Evaluation Logic
Its evaluation logic is theoretical: it identifies where XAI intersects with established IS themes such as trust, decision support, governance, and human-AI interaction.

## Performance Comparison
Instead of performance tables, the paper contributes conceptual positioning and future research questions.

## Architecture / Conceptual Explanation

Read the paper as an IS-use chain:
- `Starting point`: explanation is part of system design.
- `Core mechanism`: explanations shape how users understand and control the system.
- `Key message`: the value of explainability appears through use and accountability, not only technical transparency.
## How the Main Metrics / Evaluation Logic Work
The logic is interpretive rather than numerical: value comes from how explanations affect use, control, and decision processes.

## Relevance to the Present Project Design
This paper is valuable because it helps translate the present project from a pure technical artifact into an IS artifact concerned with decision support, transparency, and organizational use.
